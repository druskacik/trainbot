## Design Context

### Users
- **Who:** Travelers planning European night train trips (sleeper, couchette). Mix of budget-conscious planners and train enthusiasts.
- **Context:** No dominant device -- used on both desktop (planning at home) and mobile (on the go). Design must work equally well for both.
- **Job to be done:** Pick two cities, set preferences, search, get the lowest fare with a direct booking link. Fast and repeatable -- users come back to check prices, so optimize for familiarity and speed over first-time discoverability.
- **Emotional goal:** Users should feel *oriented* ("I know what to do") and *reassured* ("this is the smarter way to travel").

### Brand Personality
- **Three words:** Calm, precise, grounded.
- **Story:** TrainBot sits at the intersection of ecology and technology -- travel by train instead of plane, with smart tools to find the best route. The brand reflects that: thoughtful about the planet, sharp about the data.
- **Voice:** Quietly confident. No hype, no greenwashing. Copy is short, direct, human. The product does the work; the interface gets out of the way.
- **What we're not:** Preachy, corporate-green, or tech-bro. No fake warmth, no gradients-for-the-sake-of-it.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual tone:** Restrained, legible, plenty of space. Think seat61.com -- simple, informational, trustworthy. Every element earns its place.
- **Color:** Single accent (teal-green `#1a6b5c` light / `#2e7d6e` dark). Small palette; color supports hierarchy and actions, nothing else.
- **Typography:** Syne (display) + Plus Jakarta Sans (body). Distinctive but grounded. Typography and spacing carry the design.
- **Theme:** Light default, dark via `prefers-color-scheme`. Contrast and readability over trend.
- **Future:** Room for subtle illustrations (route maps, train silhouettes) but not a current priority. Keep the UI typographic and structural for now.
- **Anti-references:** Generic SaaS dashboards, crypto/AI landing pages, glassmorphism, cyan/indigo palettes, sustainability cliches (leaf logos, green overload). Must not look "AI-generated."
- **Positive references:** seat61.com -- simple, dense with useful info, no fluff.

### Design Principles
1. **One accent, clear hierarchy** -- Single accent for key actions and focus. Spacing and typography carry the rest. No competing colors.
2. **Token-first** -- CSS custom properties for spacing, radius, colors, focus ring, shadows. Maintainable and themable, no hardcoded values.
3. **Accessible by default** -- `:focus-visible` on all interactive elements, 44px touch targets, sufficient contrast (WCAG AA), `aria-live`/`aria-busy` for dynamic content, respect `prefers-reduced-motion`.
4. **Calm and scannable** -- The flow (From/To -> options -> Search -> results) should be obvious. Whitespace and structure over visual noise.
5. **Fast for regulars** -- Optimize for repeat visits. Familiar layout, minimal friction, no unnecessary onboarding or decoration between the user and their search.
6. **Resilient and honest** -- In-page errors (no `alert()`), safe external links (`rel="noopener noreferrer"`), clear loading states announced to assistive tech.
